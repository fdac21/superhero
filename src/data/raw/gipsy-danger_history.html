Gipsy Danger's History

Early Combat History: Gipsy Danger was constructed on Kodiak Island at the Jaeger Academyâ€™s Jaeger testing facility. Launched on July 10, 2017, Gipsy Danger was stationed at the Anchorage Shatterdome, tasked to defend the coastline of Alaska.[3] Gipsy Danger was piloted by the Becket brothers, Yancy and Raleigh.

In 2017, Gipsy Danger fought against the Category III Kaiju Yamarashi.[3] In 2019, Gipsy Danger accompanies Lucky Seven and Horizon Brave to Manila to stop a Category IV Kaiju.[4][note 1] Prior to 2020, Gipsy Danger earned four Kaiju kills with the Becket brothers.[note 2]

Anchorage Attack: In 2020, Gipsy Danger is deployed into the Gulf of Alaska to fight the Kaiju Knifehead. They approach the Kaiju's present target, the Saltchuck, from underwater and emerge in time to save the fishing boat from certain destruction, disobeying Stacker Pentecost's order to ignore it. Gipsy Danger punches Knifehead in the face, knocking the Kaiju off balance. Raising its arms, it lands a blow on the Kaiju's head. Knifehead retaliates, catching the Jaeger's left wrist in its jaws. Gipsy Danger pushes back and grabs the side of its head. The Beckets use the Plasma Cannon and shoot Knifehead in the abdomen. Knifehead falls into the sea, presumed dead by the pilots.[3]

Their victory, however, is short-lived. The injured Kaiju reemerges from the ocean and ambushes the Jaeger. Yancy attempts to hold Knifehead back while Raleigh preps the left cannon. Knifehead blocks the attack and pierces the Jaeger's armpit. Knifehead severs the left arm from Gipsy Danger's body. Overpowering Gipsy Danger, Knifehead breaches the right side of the Conn-Pod and tears away half of Gipsy Danger's head, killing Yancy in the process.[3]

As Knifehead begins to tear away at Gipsy Danger's armor, Raleigh is able to use the right Plasma Cannon to kill the Kaiju once and for all. Raleigh is able to pilot Gipsy long enough to reach the shores of Anchorage, where the Jaeger falls. Raleigh emerges from the Conn-Pod and collapses on the beach from the near-fatal strain of piloting Gipsy Danger solo.[3]

Decommission and Restoration: Following its pyrrhic victory, Gipsy Danger is salvaged from the Anchorage beach and sent to Oblivion Bay. Around the time Anchorage Shatterdome closes in 2024, Stacker Pentecost goes against the Pan Pacific Defense Corps's desire to junk the Mark-3 Jaeger, relocating it to the shuttered Shatterdome. When reporter Naomi Sokolov arrives at the Anchorage Shatterdome to interview Pentecost, Gipsy Danger is in the process of being of airlifted to the Hong Kong Shatterdome.[note 3][5]

The Mark-3 Restoration Project is sanctioned by Pentecost, and Gipsy Dangr undergoes major reconstruction and refurbishment under the supervision of J-Tech officers Mako Mori and Tendo Choi. The Jaeger is rebuilt with new technology, a solid iron hull with no alloys, retractable chain swords, new cockpit, and updates to the control interfaces to match recent tech upgrades by the PPDC.[3]

When the United Nations officially cuts funding to the Jaeger Program, Gipsy Danger undergoes the final preparations for its last mission alongside Cherno Alpha, Crimson Typhoon, and Striker Eureka. Raleigh Becket is later recruited by Pentecost as the co-pilot of Gipsy Danger.[3]

Hong Kong Incident: Gipsy Danger is the last Jaeger to arrive on the scene in the Victoria Harbour and saves Striker Eureka after it is disabled by an EMP burst from Leatherback. Gipsy Danger engages Leatherback, drawing its attention away from the disabled Jaeger and its pilots, Herc and Chuck Hansen. Gipsy Danger tears the EMP organ from Leatherback's body and appears to gain the upper hand in the battle. However, Leatherback tosses the Jaeger across the harbor and into the port.[3]

Gipsy Danger fights to keep Leatherback from breaching the port any further. It uses its elbow rocket to keep Leatherback down. Grappling the Kaiju from behind, Gipsy Danger continues to push Leatherback back toward the water. Locked in a power struggle, Gipsy Danger amputates Leatherback's arm with the Plasma Cannon, killing the Kaiju with several shots to its side. Raleigh decides to "check for a pulse" by unloading another volley of shots into its body, making sure that it's really dead. Once the Kaiju's death is confirmed, Gipsy Danger enters the city armed with an oil tanker.[3]

Gipsy Danger fights Otachi with the tanker until the Kaiju tears the ship from its grasp, and tosses it aside. Lashing out with its tail, Otachi uses the city buildings to evade detection. During the search, Gipsy Danger is ambushed by the Kaiju and smashed through the adjacent buildings. Otachi spits acid at the Jaeger, but Gipsy Danger avoids the attack and the acid disintegrates part of a building.[3] Mako uses the coolant from Gipsy Danger's ventilation system to freeze Otachi's tail and shatter it when the Kaiju tries to crush its head.

Gipsy Danger tears out Otachi's acid sack and tosses it away. Otachi digs its talons into Gipsy Danger and unfurls her wings, taking off with Gipsy Danger in tow. After several unsuccessful attempts at freeing themselves, Mako engages Gipsy Danger's Chain Sword and bisects Otachi above Earth's atmosphere. Gipsy Danger plummets to the ground at increasing speed but lands safely in a stadium when it purges its nuclear reactor to slow its descent and loosens all the shock absorbers on Marshal Pentecost's instruction.[3]

Destroying the Breach: After the Rangers regroup, Gipsy Danger and Striker Eureka are taken to the Marianas Trench to assault the Breach. The two Jaegers are cut off by the arrival of Raiju, Scunner and Slattern. Scunner and Raiju separate Striker Eureka and Gipsy Danger. Gipsy Danger fights Scunner after the Kaiju ambushes it from the darkness. The Jaeger overpowers the Kaiju and prepares to kill it with its Chain Sword when its right arm is torn off by Raiju.

Scunner uses Gipsy Danger's momentary vulnerability to its advantage and tears at its leg. Mako and Raleigh stab the Kaiju in the head and drag it over a volcanic pit to burn its head. Scunner breaks away from the Jaeger as Raiju begins its re-approach. Gipsy Danger uses its left chain sword to bisect Raiju.[3]

Slattern calls for Scunner's assistance, giving Gipsy Danger momentary reprieve. Raleigh and Mako attempt to help Striker Eureka, but Pentecost warns them away, ordering them to use Gipsy Danger's nuclear reactor to destroy the Breach. Striker Eureka uses the nuclear bomb strapped to its back to kill Scunner and wound Slattern. Gipsy Danger crouches low to the ground and braces itself with the chain sword to withstand the explosion wave that parts the sea and creates an air bubble. As the water rushes back, the Jaeger is swamped by turbulent water. Mako and Raleigh recover, but not without sustaining heavy damage to Gipsy Danger's operating systems.[3]

Gipsy Danger drags Raiju's corpse and limps for the portal. They are momentarily halted by Slattern, grievously wounded by the nuclear explosion. Gipsy Danger tackles Slattern and stabs him. As they both enter the portal, Gipsy kills Slattern using a point-blank blast from the nuclear vortex turbine. The Kaiju's body allows them to enter the Breach.[3]

As Mako's life support systems begin to fail, Raleigh ejects her from the Jaeger. He manually arms Gipsy's nuclear reactor to self-destruct and moves to escape in his own pod. The Precursors watch as Gipsy Danger descends down toward them and explodes, destroying the Kaiju creation facility and collapsing the Breach.[3]
Superhero Class

The Superhero Database Classification number, or SHDB Class, is a number that represents the overall 'power' of a character. All traits of a character are used for calculating the Classification.

What it DOESN'T mean

This doesn't mean that a higher class would always beat a lower class character. But the bigger the difference in Class is, the more obvious it is who'll win in a fight.

How is this calculated
( INT^1.3 + (STR*0.5 )^2 + (SPE*0.5)^2 + DUR^1.6 + (POW + (SPS*SPL))^2 + COM^1.8 ) ^ TIER
Super Power Score and Level

Every Super Power has a score (SPS) that is used to calculate the Class. Each Super Power also has 3 levels (SPL). The level is set when connecting that Super Power to a character. The level determines the final score, of the Super Power, being used in the calculation.
